Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

EQ refers to a person’s ability to understand his/her emotion along with other person’s emotions. Emotion intelligent consists of five elements.

  • Self-Awareness- To be aware of your own emotion and what you are going through, you will be in a better position to understand others. This also means you are aware of your strength and weakness.
  • Self- regulation- Think before you speak. This helps to avoid any negativity when conversing with other people and will create more positive outcome.
  • Motivation- Being self motivated is one of the traits of EI individuals. Money or prestigious titles are not motivating factors for them. Being driven to pursue goals for personal reasons, rather than for some kind of rewards (external motivation). Leading by example of how things can be done. Even when they are faced with challenges they try finding something good in that situation and not easily disappointed
  • Empathy- When you are able to put yourself in other person shoes and think about a situation is known as empathy.
  • Social skills- having great social skills will help to communicate effectively to other people. The ability to manage relationships and build networks.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ is basically comparing the level of intelligence possess by an individual whereas EQ is about controlling your emotion as well as other people emotions.

IQ is General Intelligence and EQ is Emotional Intelligence.

IQ measure how individual learn, understand and apply information whereas EQ measure how individual learn, understand and apply emotional knowledge.

Why is it important?

People with high EI are better at motivating themselves. EQ determine how you interact and treat people in your life, it shows how you are going to cope up with pressures or face the crisis. They are also better at identifying and empathising with other people’s points of view – an essential trait for preventing and resolving conflicts at work. With a better understanding of our emotions, we can communicate our feelings in a more positive manner. We can also understand and relate to our colleagues, which can lead to better working relationships. EQ has a significant impact on job performance, interview skills, first impressions, salesmanship, as well as other aspects of business commonly associated with a rising star in the workplace.
